Paintings of bodies, rooms, and streets where the air feels slightly off.

I work from observation, memory, and accident. Figures hunch, reach, or dissolve into the space around them; heads become bowls, coats stand in for missing bodies, architecture tilts into shadow. The surfaces are reworked and scraped back—nothing is too fixed, everything is in process.

Bodies under pressure

Many of the paintings begin with the figure: a back turned away, a crouched body on a bed, someone leaning into the dark. I’m interested less in likeness and more in posture—how a body can read as tired, alert, devotional, or absent with just a few marks.

Rooms, streets, and headspace

Other works drift toward interiors and city scenes: doorways, corridors, figures slipping in and out of frame. Sometimes the head becomes a container or a landscape. The paintings sit between narrative and abstraction, like a half-remembered moment you can’t quite place.

How commissions work

I take on a small number of commissions each year. The work stays close to my studio practice—gestural, slightly uneasy, somewhere between figure and space. Think of it as inviting a particular kind of painting into your life, rather than ordering an illustration.

Small From $1,500
~18 × 14 in. Often an intimate figure, head, or fragment of a room. Good for apartments, hallways, and bedside walls.
Medium From $3,200
~30 × 24 in. A more developed situation: a body in space, a doorway, a table, or a night scene. We talk about mood, palette, and what should feel present or absent.
Large From $5,500
40 in and up. For statement walls or public-facing spaces. These works tend to be slower, layered, and more architectural. Timeline, framing, and installation are discussed individually.

I don’t take on photoreal copies of photos or “match the sofa” projects—but I’m happy to translate a story, a room, or a recurring dream into this language of paint.

About the work

Enough context to orient you; the rest is in the paintings.

I’m a painter living and working in New York City, currently completing my MFA. The work grows out of long sessions with the figure, drawings in the street, and a lot of repainting. I’m drawn to awkward poses, half-lit corners, and images that feel like they’re arriving and erasing themselves at the same time. Surfaces are built slowly: thin scrapes of color next to thick ridges of paint, muddy browns against sour greens, bruised pinks and asphalt blues. I’m interested in how much a scene can be reduced before it falls apart—and how far it can be pushed without losing the sense of a body or a place. This site is intentionally tight: a handful of paintings that feel representative of the current studio, clear information for collectors, and a direct way to get in touch.
